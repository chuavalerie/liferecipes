Ask Me

This is a million dollar question. Honestly, I don't think there's a real reason to live — we're just passersby in this metaverse of life. But there's no reason not to live, either. So I guess we just make do with what we have and enjoy it while we can.

I think it boils down to being grateful and counting our blessings. I don’t do this well all the time, but I try when I can. Sometimes, you look back and realize that the things that mattered then don’t really matter at all. We just don’t know it yet. We are mere dust in this vast world. I guess that’s why I love nature — it reminds me how fragile life is and how insignificant my worries can be.

Lots of things, in particular — nature, sunlight, the people I love, archaeology, history, running, chick flicks, comedies — and the list goes on. Having a wonderful conversation with someone, or simply feeling that life is great, makes me happy too.
